Introduction

Soldering is a useful skill. It allows you to create strong electronic connections using molten metal (solder). When done correctly, it creates reliable electrical joints used in almost all electronics.

Tools Required

Soldering does not need the most expensive equipment. A basic setup includes:
  • Soldering iron
  • Solder (lead-free recommended)
  • Soldering iron stand
  • Flux (helps solder flow properly)
Optional but very useful tools:
  • Solder wick (removes excess solder)
  • Solder sucker (desoldering pump)
  • Helping hands clamp
  • Brass wool or sponge for cleaning the iron tip

Safety

Safety is very important when soldering.
  • Always work in a well-ventilated area to avoid inhaling fumes
  • The soldering iron tip gets extremely hot (around 300°C+)
  • Never touch the metal tip while powered
  • Always return the iron to its stand when not in use
  • Wear safety glasses to protect from solder splashes
  • Wash hands after handling solder or electronics
  • Avoid eating or drinking near soldering areas
Soldering is safe when done carefully, but careless handling can cause burns or injury.

Basic Steps

  1. Prepare your workspace and tools
  2. Heat the soldering iron
  3. Clean the iron tip
  4. Place the component in position
  5. Heat both pad and wire together
  6. Apply solder to the joint (not the iron)
  7. Let it cool naturally without movement
  8. Inspect the joint for a shiny, solid connection

Troubleshooting

Common issues include:
  • Cold joints (dull, weak connections)
  • Not enough heat
  • Dirty or oxidised surfaces
  • Too much solder applied
  • Moving parts while solder cools
Fixes:
  • Reheat and reflow the joint
  • Clean surfaces before soldering
  • Use flux to improve flow
  • Keep components steady while cooling
